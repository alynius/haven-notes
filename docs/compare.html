How Haven is different.

vs Notion

Notion is a workspace. Haven is a notebook. Notion is incredible for teams, databases, and structured project management — but that power comes with overhead. You're managing pages inside pages, setting up templates, and navigating a block-based editor that treats every paragraph as an object. Haven strips all of that away. Open it and write. Your notes are local, private, and never at risk if a company changes its pricing.

vs Bear

Bear is beautiful and we admire it. Haven adds wiki links and a knowledge graph to that simplicity — letting you build connections between notes and see how your ideas relate over time. Haven also ships E2E encryption for sync, so your notes stay private even in transit. If you love Bear's writing experience but want your notes to form a connected web of knowledge, Haven is where to look.

vs Apple Notes

Apple Notes is simple and reliable. Haven adds markdown formatting, wiki links, a knowledge graph, and end-to-end encrypted sync that you control. Apple Notes ties your data to iCloud; Haven lets you write without an account and sync on your own terms. It's also built for people who think in plain text and want their writing environment to stay out of the way.

vs Obsidian

Obsidian is powerful and extensible. Haven is fast and native. Obsidian's mobile app is a wrapped web view; Haven is written in SwiftUI and feels at home on iPhone. Haven has no plugin ecosystem — that's intentional. The features that matter are built in and polished. If you want a vault with hundreds of plugins, Obsidian delivers. If you want a private, snappy, beautifully native notes app that just works, Haven is it.
